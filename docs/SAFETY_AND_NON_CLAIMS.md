# Safety and non-claims

## Non-claims

IX-Liquid is not represented here as:
- a drinking-water machine
- a universal contaminant destroyer
- a PFAS destruction system
- a permit exemption
- an autonomous system that should be deployed without site-specific review
- a replacement for qualified environmental, mechanical, electrical, or controls engineering

## Hazard posture

Even a small pilot skid can create real hazards:
- electrical shock
- rotating machinery / pump hazards
- contaminated liquid exposure
- hydrocarbon waste handling
- pressurized lines and vessels
- UV exposure if a UV-C stage is installed
- confined cleanout or waste handling mistakes
- slip hazards from leaks or maintenance spills

## Safety design rules

1. Keep treatment and evidence alive long enough to explain the failure.
2. Prefer blocked discharge over unverified discharge.
3. Put service access ahead of cosmetic compactness.
4. Treat every sensor as fallible; stale data is not valid data.
5. Separate contamination handling from normal drain paths.
6. Label every valve and every service isolation point.
7. Use secondary containment under leak-prone zones.
8. Force manual acknowledgement after major safety events.

## Required human controls

Before field use:
- site hazard review
- liquid characterization plan
- waste disposal plan
- lockout/tagout procedure
- electrical review
- operator training
- emergency contact and escalation path

## Deployment limits

Do not deploy this pilot as-is for:
- explosive atmospheres without proper rated hardware
- potable-water claims
- untreated sewage
- unknown reactive waste streams
- high-salinity or high-temperature service beyond component ratings
- unattended long-duration operation without communications and inspection plans
